\chapter{Results}
\label{sec:results}
Metrics are reported following the conventions described in \autoref{sec:method}. For convenience, we often refer to reconstruction methods as PriorName-SampleName. Figures shorten PaDIS-VE-DPS to simply PaDIS.

\section{Reconstruction Quality}

The main inverse-solver comparison results are presented in \autoref{tab:main-quality}. At 20 views, the validation-selected LION-Physical PaDIS-VE-DDNM configuration achieved the highest PSNR ($36.49\,\mathrm{dB}$), while fixed patch averaging achieved the highest SSIM and lowest MAE on its smaller four-image subset. At 8 views, whole-image VE-DPS produced the highest PSNR ($29.58\,\mathrm{dB}$), while patch averaging again led SSIM and MAE on four images. PaDIS-DPS improved substantially over FDK and CP-TV in both settings, but did not exceed whole-image diffusion within the VE-DPS comparison, for which each prior used its own validation-selected settings. Operator-normalised LION-Physical DPS reduced the relative sinogram residual to approximately $10^{-3}$, over an order of magnitude below the Paper-Faithful and Repo-Faithful DPS rows. The representative rows in \autoref{fig:results-ct-reconstruction} and the extended 20- and 8-view panels in \autoref{fig:results-ct20-additional} and \autoref{fig:results-ct8-additional} show that these trends persist across varied slices rather than one favourable example.

We observed the same sampler dependence across geometries (\autoref{tab:extra-quality} and \autoref{fig:results-extra-ct}), with PaDIS-VE-DDNM reconstruction leading the 60-view experiment at $41.55\,\mathrm{dB}$ and $0.951$ SSIM. In the 120-degree limited-angle problem, whole-image VE-DPS gave the best image metrics, whereas PaDIS-VE-DPS gave the smallest residual. At native resolution, LION-Physical PaDIS-VE-DPS reconstruction reached $40.08\,\mathrm{dB}$ and $0.960$ SSIM (\autoref{tab:ct512} and \autoref{fig:results-512-ct}), improving on CP-TV by $6.31\,\mathrm{dB}$.

\section{Ablations}

\subsection{Patch Size}

Our patch-size analysis in \autoref{tab:patch-size} did not show a simple small-patch advantage. The $96\times96$ model achieved $34.26\,\mathrm{dB}$, almost identical in mean PSNR to the $34.25\,\mathrm{dB}$ whole-image result, whereas $32\times32$ patches performed worst. Very small $8\times8$ patches recovered much of the lost performance, so patch area alone cannot explain the non-monotonic trend. The extended visual comparison in \autoref{fig:results-patch-size} suggests that the larger patches preserve fine anatomy more consistently. As the non-default patch models inherited the $P=56$ reconstruction setting rather than being tuned separately, these rows should not be interpreted as model-specific optima.

\subsection{Position, Initialisation and Schedule}

Replacing FDK initialisation with Gaussian noise changed LION-Physical PSNR by at most $0.10\,\mathrm{dB}$ within matched geometric-schedule rows (\autoref{tab:position}), while removing positional channels reduced PSNR by $0.30\,\mathrm{dB}$ from FDK and $0.40\,\mathrm{dB}$ from noise. The reconstructions in \autoref{fig:results-position-encoding} are nevertheless visually similar. Positional channels therefore gave only a small improvement in these measurement-conditioned reconstructions, although the no-position model inherited the default model's inference setting and this experiment does not test position-free unconditional generation. In contrast, replacing the geometric schedule with the public repository's EDM-style default reduced the position-enabled LION-Physical result from $32.82$ to $31.53\,\mathrm{dB}$ with FDK initialisation.

\subsection{Sampler Dependence}

Sampler choice changed the ranking of the learned priors. Under their validation-selected LION-Physical settings at 20 views, VE-DDNM favoured PaDIS by $2.30\,\mathrm{dB}$, whilst VE-DPS and Langevin favoured the whole-image prior by $1.43$ and $1.64\,\mathrm{dB}$ respectively (\autoref{tab:main-quality}). Predictor-corrector gave similar PSNR for both priors but substantially higher SSIM for PaDIS. The comparison in \autoref{fig:results-sampling-methods} shows that each method produced plausible low-frequency anatomy on the selected slice, even where quantitative performance differed. As we selected settings per prior and sampler, these comparisons show the best observed configurations rather than isolating the patch score alone.

\section{Runtime and Unconditional Generation}

We recorded approximately two minutes per slice for PaDIS-DPS, $24\%$ less than whole-image VE-DPS, while fixed patch averaging and stitching took about $24$ minutes (\autoref{tab:runtime}). These jobs could run concurrently and were not isolated timing benchmarks. Among the four untuned samples per method in \autoref{fig:results-generation}, random PaDIS partitions avoided the rectangular discontinuities visible under fixed stitching, although this small sample is insufficient to rank distributional quality.
